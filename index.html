PnL Guide

Calculation notes

Option Lifecycle Matching

Option closes match the exact contract first. If an order closes one contract and opens another, v2 links it as a roll and keeps the PnL in the same strategy lifecycle.

Cashflow PnL

Sum of CSV Total by ticker, including trade cashflows, fees, commissions, dividends, expirations, assignments, Wire Funds Received, and funds withdrawals. Example: sell option premium $100 minus $1 commission and $0.13 fee contributes $98.87 cashflow.

Realized PnL

Closed-lot PnL from FIFO matching using net CSV Total values, so commissions and fees are included. Example: buy 10 shares for $500 total and later sell those 10 for $620 total gives $120 realized PnL.

Cumulative Cashflow

Running monthly sum of all CSV Total values. Example: January cashflow $200 followed by February -$50 shows cumulative values of $200 then $150.

FIFO Closed Lots

First-in, first-out means the earliest open lot is matched first when a closing trade appears. Open/unmatched lots remain separate and are not marked to market.

Normalized Ticker

The dashboard groups rows by Underlying Symbol, then Root Symbol, then Symbol. This turns option contracts like RGTI 260918C00017000 into RGTI so option, equity, assignment, dividend, and fee rows can be reviewed together.

Wheel Definition

In v3, a wheel is assignment-anchored: short puts and rolls that lead to assignment, then assigned stock, covered calls, dividends/interest, and stock sale or call-away exit. Short puts that never become assigned stock stay in option analysis, not Wheel.

Stock Split Handling

Forward and reverse split Receive Deliver rows are treated as position transformations. They do not create realized PnL; the share count is converted from the close row to the open row while total cost basis is preserved.
